About Me

I am a senior at the University of Texas at San Antonio graduating this upcoming Spring. As my studies come to an end, I realize that my journey into the world of technology and computer science has only just begun. I will leverage the knowledge I have gained at school to acquire new skills, opportunities, and ultimately, contribute to something bigger than myself.

Throughout my studies I explored areas such as data science, artificial intelligence, computation theory, web development, and more. I am grateful for the wide diversity of classes I have taken, but as I begin my career, I aim to refine and specialize my skill set.

I have created clean, well-designed code for the past two years in mainly Python, C, and Java. I've used Java to learn different design patterns in my object oriented systems course in conjunction with my secure software development course shifting my perspective on the software development life cycle.

I am now able to think about systems at a higher level of abstraction than code implementation, designing solutions that fit the problem domain. This allows not only an easier development process, but also for things like scalability, reusability, and most importantly, the security of systems and their components.

I want to help develop secure, well-maintained, software. The “secure” aspect of this is important to me as I have been slowly delving into cybersecurity and threat intelligence. The countless ways threat actors exploit individuals or organizations creates a dynamic, volatile cyberspace, demanding vigilance and innovation.
