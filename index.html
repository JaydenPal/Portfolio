About Me

I am currently a senior at the University of Texas at San Antonio expecting to graduate this upcoming Spring. As my studies come to an end, I am realizing that my journey into the world of computer science and technology is only just beginning. I am eager to to leverage the knowledge I have gained at school in order to acquire new skills, opportunities, and to ultimately contribute to something bigger than myself.

Throughout my studies I have explored areas such as data science, artificial intelligence, computational theory, web development, and a multitude of others. I am extremely grateful for the wide diversity of classes I have been able to take but as I begin my career, I am aiming to refine and specialize my skill set.

I have been creating clean, well-designed code for the past two years in mainly Java, Python, and C. I have experience in other languages as well but these are what I have used for the majority of my work. Recently, I have used Java to learn different design patterns in my object oriented systems course. This is conjunction with my secure software development class have shifted my perspective when it comes to creating programs.

My approach is far more organized than when I was first learning to program. I am able to think about systems at a higher level, focusing not just on code implementation but designing solutions that fit the problem domain. This allows not only an easier development process, but also for things like scalability, reusability, and most importantly, the security of systems or their components.

I want to help develop secure, well-maintained, software. The “secure” aspect of this is important to me as I have been slowly delving into cybersecurity and threat intelligence. The countless ways threat actors can exploit individuals or organizations creates a dynamic, volatile landscape in the cyberspace that demands vigilance and innovation.
